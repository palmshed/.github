# Get Started With Your Application

Before submitting your application, please ensure that you meet the eligibility requirements and have prepared the necessary documentation. Applications missing required information may not be approved.

## Before You Apply

Make sure all of the following are true:

* You are the **administrator or owner** of the GitHub organization on behalf of which you are applying.
* You have gathered **all required nonprofit documentation**, such as tax records or legal certification.
* You have reviewed the **nonprofit eligibility criteria** outlined below.
* You have **verified the GitHub organization** you are applying for.
* Your **affiliation with the organization is clearly documented**.

## Nonprofit Eligibility

Your entity must qualify as a nonprofit organization.

Eligible organizations are:

* Registered as **501(c)(3)** or an equivalent nonprofit entity (outside the U.S.)
* **Non-governmental**
* **Non-academic**
* **Non-commercial**
* **Non-political**

Organizations that do not meet these criteria may not be approved.

## Required Application Information

To verify your nonprofit status, your application must include the following:

* **Proof of nonprofit status** issued by your local government

  * (e.g., 501(c)(3) documentation in the United States)
* The **registered legal name** of your nonprofit
* A **brief summary** describing what your organization does
* Explicit confirmation that your organization is:

  * Nongovernmental
  * Nonacademic
  * Noncommercial
  * Nonpolitical

Incomplete or unclear submissions may delay or prevent approval.

## Troubleshooting

If you do not see your organization listed in the dropdown when applying for a nonprofit discount, try the following steps:

1. **Remove and re-enable GitHub OAuth**

   * Go to your GitHub profile settings.
   * Remove the GitHub authorization for *GitHub for Nonprofits* associated with your organizations.
2. Return to the **GitHub for Nonprofits** page and reauthorize access.

   * Be sure to select the organization you want to verify.
3. Confirm that your organization **allows OAuth access** in its settings.

## Additional Notes (Strongly Recommended)

Help us review your application efficiently:

* Use the **Additional Notes** section to describe your organization in more detail.
* Include links to **relevant GitHub repositories**.
* Explain **how you are currently using GitHub** or how you plan to use it.
* Share any other context that helps us understand your mission and technical work.
